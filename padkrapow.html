Pad krapow

Pad Krapow, a beloved Thai dish, is a flavorful stir-fry featuring minced meat, often chicken or pork, infused with aromatic Thai basil leaves and a spicy sauce. Served over steamed rice and topped with a fried egg, Pad Krapow offers a perfect harmony of heat, savory richness, and herbal freshness.

Ingredients

  • Ground chicken or pork
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Thai bird chilies (or other spicy chilies, finely chopped)
  • Cooking oil
  • Soy sauce
  • Oyster sauce
  • Fish sauce
  • Sugar
  • Fresh Thai basil leaves
  • Optional: Bell peppers (sliced), onion (sliced) and/or bamboo shoots
  • Fried eggs for serving (optional)

Steps

Prepare Ground Meet

  1. Ground chicken or pork
  2. Minced garlic
  3. Finely chopped Thai bird chilies

Heat the pan

  1. Add cooking oil to a wok or large skillet. Heat over medium-high heat.

Sauté Aromatics

  1. Add minced garlic and chopped Thai bird chilies to the hot oil. Sauté until fragrant, approximately 30 seconds.

Cook Ground Meat

  1. Add the ground chicken or pork to the pan. Break it apart and cook until browned and fully cooked

Seasoning

  1. Season the meat with soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, and sugar. Adjust quantities based on taste preferences.

Optional Vegetables

  1. If desired, add sliced bell peppers, onions, or bamboo shoots. Stir-fry until vegetables are slightly tender.

Add Fresh Thai Basil

  1. Introduce a generous amount of fresh Thai basil leaves to the pan. Stir until the basil wilts and imparts its aromatic flavor.

Taste and Adjust

  1. Sample the dish and adjust seasoning if necessary. You can add more soy sauce, fish sauce, or sugar to achieve the desired balance.

Serve

  1. Present Pad Krapow over steamed rice.
  2. Optionally, top each serving with a fried egg.
