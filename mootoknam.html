Moo tok nam

Moo Tok Nam, a delightful Thai dish, showcases marinated and grilled pork collar that is thinly sliced and served with a zesty dipping sauce. This succulent and flavorful dish offers a perfect balance of smokiness from the grill, tenderness in the meat, and a burst of tangy goodness from the accompanying dipping sauce.

Ingredients

Marinade for Grilled Pork

  • Pork collar or pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Soy sauce
  • Oyster sauce
  • Sugar
  • White pepper
  • Sesame oil

Dipping sauce

  • Lime juice
  • Fish sauce
  • Thai bird chilies (finely chopped)
  • Garic (minced)
  • Sugar
  • Optional: Fresh cilantro (chopped) for garnish

Steps

Marinade for Grilled Pork

  1. In a bowl, mix thinly sliced pork collar or pork shoulder with minced garlic, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, white pepper, and a dash of sesame oil.
  2. Ensure the pork slices are well coated with the marinade, then cover the bowl and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.

Dipping Sauce

  1. In a separate bowl, prepare the dipping sauce by combining lime juice, fish sauce, finely chopped Thai bird chilies, minced garlic, and sugar. Adjust the quantities to achieve a balanced sweet, sour, salty, and spicy flavor.
  2. Stir the sauce until the sugar is dissolved, and set it aside.

Grilling

  1. Heat a grill or grill pan over medium-high heat.
  2. Grill the marinated pork slices until they are cooked through and develop a nice char on the edges. This usually takes about 2-3 minutes per side.
  3. Once grilled, remove the pork from the heat.

Serving

  1. Arrange the grilled pork slices on a serving platter.
  2. Drizzle some of the dipping sauce over the pork or serve the sauce on the side.
  3. Optionally, garnish with fresh chopped cilantro.
